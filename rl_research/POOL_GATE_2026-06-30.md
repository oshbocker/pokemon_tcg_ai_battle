# Promotion gate: mirror → pool (2026-06-30)

## Change

`scripts/train_selfplay.py` gained `--gate-vs {mirror,pool}` (default `mirror`, backward-compat).
The old gate promoted `best.pt` by playing the current net against a frozen copy of its own
last-promoted best (`play_match(model, frozen_best, deck)`) — a **pure mirror**. That number can be
maximised while the shipped checkpoint stays blind to the decks it actually faces on the ladder
(see [`RL_LADDER_ANALYSIS_2026-06-30.md`](./RL_LADDER_ANALYSIS_2026-06-30.md): mirror 9-3, but
Alakazam 0-3 and Starmie 0-2).

`--gate-vs pool` crowns `best.pt` by the current net's **weighted win-rate across the whole training
league**, the same opponents we train against:

- **self** (frozen last-best, weight `--w-self`) and a **past** bucket — a sample of recent frozen
  checkpoints from the league pool (`--gate-past`, combined weight `--w-past`) — both via
  `play_match` on the mirror deck. Preserves the "don't regress vs your past self" anti-collapse
  signal (the frozen best is itself part of the pool).
- the **manifest agents** (`agent/opponents/mixed_pool*.json`: kaggle:*, heuristic, random, their own
  weights) via `quick_eval` / `_resolve_eval_opponent` — each pilots its own deck, side-swapped.

Aggregate = weighted mean of per-opponent win-rates (NaN buckets dropped). The log line prints the
**per-opponent breakdown**, e.g.

```
it 275 ... | gate pool 58% [self 61 | past 55 | starmie 41 | dragapult 47 | heuristic 70 | random 88]  [PROMOTED]
```

## Promotion rule (recalibrated)

The mirror gate compared a single win-rate to a fixed `--gate-threshold` (~0.55–0.65). A weighted
pool win-rate against strong rule agents sits lower and on a different scale, so the pool gate is
**relative**: promote when the current pool score **exceeds the last-promoted checkpoint's** pool
score (stored in `best.pt` as `gate_pool_score`, monotonic). `--gate-threshold` becomes an optional
**absolute floor** (0 = off) on top of that. `--gate-games` is now **per opponent bucket**.

## Scope / non-goals

Only *which checkpoint is crowned* changed — training, the league, and the reward are untouched.
Deck stays controlled: trainee deck for self/past, kaggle:* pilot their own siblings. `kaggle:archaludon`
stays held OUT of the training/gate manifest so the `eval:` column remains an honest yardstick.

## Validation

Tiny CPU smoke (`--size tiny --gate-vs pool --gate-every 1 --gate-games 2`) confirms the per-opponent
breakdown prints, the `past` bucket is cleanly dropped when the pool is empty, the absolute floor
blocks an untrained net, and the relative gate promotes + stores `gate_pool_score` in `best.pt`.
`scripts/prepare.py` green (format/lint/pyright/pytest). Colab long-run cell switched to
`--gate-vs pool` (floor 0.4). See [[asymmetric-selfplay-infra]].

## Future ideas

- **Difficulty-ramped opponent weights (curriculum).** Today every league weight is *static* —
  only LR and entropy anneal; a fixed-weight opponent keeps a constant sampling share for the whole
  run. For a **strong, frozen** opponent (e.g. the trained Archaludon `best.pt` dropped into the
  Alakazam league via Option A), exposure risk is *front-loaded*: a frozen net is hardest for the
  trainee early (when it's weakest and most needs winnable games) and gets progressively more
  winnable as the trainee improves. A **linear weight ramp** on just that entry — start low to
  protect early reward signal, raise it as the trainee can trade with it — would match the
  "don't over-expose early" goal better than a flat weight. Deferred for v1 (constant low weight
  ships instead): it's another blind-tuned knob, cutting against the throughput-over-fiddling
  lesson, and the frozen-gets-easier dynamic means a flat low dose already guarantees signal
  throughout. Revisit if a gate breakdown shows the trainee stuck losing to a specific strong
  bucket. Would need a per-opponent weight scheduler in the league builder (`build_league`), which
  doesn't exist yet. Generalizes to any "more difficult agent" we add to a pool.
